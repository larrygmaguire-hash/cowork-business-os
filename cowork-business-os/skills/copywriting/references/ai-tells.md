# AI Writing Tells

Patterns that reveal AI-generated content. Run every piece of company-voice content against this list before presenting.

## Banned Words and Phrases

These words appear disproportionately in AI-generated text. Replace with specific, concrete alternatives.

### Vague Intensifiers
- delve, dive into, unpack
- landscape, realm, arena
- myriad, plethora, abundance
- robust, holistic, comprehensive
- streamline, optimise, leverage
- paradigm, synergy, ecosystem (unless literally describing an ecosystem)
- elevate, amplify, supercharge

### Filler Transitions (as paragraph openers)
- However,
- Moreover,
- Furthermore,
- Additionally,
- Interestingly,
- Notably,
- It is important to note that
- It is worth mentioning that
- In today's fast-paced world
- In an era of

### Performative Language
- Let's explore...
- Let's dive in...
- Let's unpack this...
- Let's break this down...
- Buckle up...
- Here's the thing:
- The truth is...
- The reality is...

### False Profundity
- At its core...
- At the end of the day...
- When all is said and done...
- The bottom line is...
- It goes without saying...

## Banned Structural Patterns

### The Five-Paragraph Essay
AI defaults to: intro paragraph, 3 body paragraphs, conclusion. Real writing does not follow this structure. Break it.

### Mechanical Topic Sentences
AI starts every section with a topic sentence that summarises what the section will say. Real writing does not announce itself. Start with a fact, a scene, or an argument.

### Mechanical Parallelism
AI loves three parallel items: "It's fast, it's reliable, and it's affordable." Occasional parallel structure is fine. Three consecutive parallel constructions is an AI tell.

### Rule of Three as Default
AI defaults to listing three things. Sometimes two is enough. Sometimes four is better. Match the content, not a formula.

### Summary Paragraphs
AI ends every section with a paragraph restating what was just said. Cut these entirely. If the section was clear, no summary is needed.

## Sentence-Level Rules

### Sentence Length Variation
AI tends to produce sentences of uniform length (15-20 words each). Real writing varies. Follow a long, complex sentence with a short, direct one. Then a medium one. The rhythm should feel spoken, not generated.

### Synonym Rotation
AI rotates synonyms to avoid repetition: "the tool", "the platform", "the solution", "the system". Pick one term and stick with it. Readers prefer clarity over variety.

### Passive Voice
AI overuses passive constructions. Max 10% of sentences should be passive. Rewrite "Reports are generated by the system" as "The system generates reports."

### Exclamation Points
Remove them. Professional writing does not need them.

## The Test

Before finalising any company-voice content, ask:

1. Could any company in any industry have written this? If yes, it is too generic. Add specificity.
2. Does every sentence earn its place? If you removed it, would the piece suffer? If not, cut it.
3. Is there a specific detail that only someone with direct experience could know? If not, the content lacks authenticity.
4. Read it aloud. Does it sound like a person talking, or a language model generating? If the latter, rewrite the flat sections.

## What Good Writing Sounds Like

- Uneven sentence lengths
- Specific details (names, numbers, places, dates)
- Opinions stated directly, not hedged to death
- Occasional incomplete thoughts that the next sentence completes
- References to real events, not hypothetical scenarios
- The writer's actual perspective, not a balanced summary of all perspectives
