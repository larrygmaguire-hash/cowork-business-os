# Landing Page Framework

StoryBrand SB7 framework for landing pages and sales pages.

## StoryBrand SB7 Overview

Every effective landing page tells a story with the customer as the hero:

1. **A character** (the customer) has a **problem**
2. And meets a **guide** (you) who gives them a **plan**
3. And calls them to **action**
4. That ends in **success** and helps them avoid **failure**

## Hero Section

The hero section (above the fold) must answer three questions in under 5 seconds:

1. **What do you offer?** (headline)
2. **How will it make my life better?** (sub-headline)
3. **What do I do next?** (CTA button)

**Headline formula:** [Outcome the customer wants] + [Without the thing they want to avoid]

**Sub-headline:** Expand on the headline with specificity. One sentence.

**CTA:** Specific action verb + what they get. "Start Your Free Trial" not "Get Started".

## 9-Section Body Structure

### 1. Problem
Name the external problem (what is going wrong), the internal problem (how it makes them feel), and the philosophical problem (why it is just wrong). The internal problem is the most powerful.

### 2. Agitation
Make the problem concrete. What does it cost them? Time, money, reputation, stress. Use specific numbers where possible.

### 3. Guide Introduction
Position yourself as the guide, not the hero. Show empathy ("We understand...") and authority ("We have helped [number] of [audience] achieve [result]").

### 4. The Plan
Show 3-4 clear steps. Number them. Each step should be one sentence. The reader should think "I can do this."

Example:
1. Sign up for a free trial
2. Connect your existing tools
3. Get your first report in 10 minutes

### 5. Deliverables / What You Get
List what is included. Be specific. Features are acceptable here because the emotional work is already done above.

### 6. Social Proof
Testimonials, logos, case study snippets, numbers. Real names and roles. Photos if possible. Specific results ("Reduced reporting time from 4 hours to 15 minutes") over vague praise ("Great product!").

### 7. FAQ
Address the 3-5 most common objections as questions. Each answer should be 2-3 sentences. End each answer on a positive note.

### 8. Final CTA
Repeat the primary CTA. Add a secondary, lower-commitment option if appropriate ("Not ready? Download our free guide instead").

### 9. Footer
Standard footer: links, legal, contact.

## Page Length Guidelines

| Offer Price | Recommended Length | Why |
|-------------|-------------------|-----|
| Free (lead magnet) | Short (hero + 2-3 sections + CTA) | Low commitment, don't oversell |
| Under 200 | Medium (hero + 5-6 sections) | Some trust-building needed |
| 200-1,000 | Full (all 9 sections) | Reader needs evidence and plan |
| Over 1,000 | Full + extended social proof and FAQ | High commitment, address every objection |

## Copy Tone Rules

Read `.claude/company/brand.md` and `.claude/company/voice.md` for tone. General landing page rules:

- Direct. No hedging.
- Specific. Numbers over adjectives.
- Benefits first, features second.
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max on landing pages).
- One idea per section.
- No jargon unless the audience expects it.
