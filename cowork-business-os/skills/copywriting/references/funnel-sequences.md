# Funnel Sequences

Email sequences, show-up sequences, and funnel type templates.

## Funnel Type Selection

| Price Point | Recommended Funnel | Conversion Mechanism |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Free (lead magnet) | Lead magnet + nurture sequence | Download → email sequence → offer |
| Under 200 | Challenge funnel | Multi-day challenge → pitch on final day |
| 200-1,000 | Webinar or VSL funnel | Registration → show-up sequence → pitch in session |
| Over 1,000 | Book-a-call funnel | Application → qualification → sales call |

## Lead Magnet Nurture Sequence (5 emails)

### Email 1: Delivery (immediately)
- Subject: "Here is your [lead magnet name]"
- Deliver the resource
- One sentence on what they will learn from it
- No selling

### Email 2: Quick Win (Day 1)
- Subject: "The fastest way to [achieve small result]"
- One actionable tip they can implement today
- Ties back to the lead magnet topic

### Email 3: Story (Day 3)
- Subject: "[Relatable situation] — sound familiar?"
- Short story illustrating the problem your product solves
- End with a question, not a pitch

### Email 4: Value (Day 5)
- Subject: "[Number] [audience] are doing this differently"
- Teach something useful. No selling yet.
- Position your expertise

### Email 5: Soft Offer (Day 7)
- Subject: "Ready to [desired outcome]?"
- Bridge from the free content to your paid offer
- Clear CTA with specific next step

## Webinar / Workshop Sequence

### Registration Confirmation (immediately)
- Confirm date, time, link
- What they will learn (3 bullet points)
- Add to calendar link

### Show-Up Email 1 (24 hours before)
- Reminder with time and link
- One compelling reason to attend

### Show-Up Email 2 (1 hour before)
- "Starting in 1 hour" with link
- Brief, urgent

### Show-Up Email 3 (at start time)
- "We are live — join now" with link only

### Post-Session Email 1 (same day)
- Thank you for attending
- Replay link (if applicable)
- Summary of key takeaways
- CTA for the offer presented

### Post-Session Email 2 (Day 2)
- Address the #1 objection from the session
- Social proof
- CTA with deadline if applicable

### Post-Session Email 3 (Day 4)
- Final reminder
- What they miss if they do not act
- Hard deadline (if genuine)

## Book-a-Call Sequence

### Application Confirmation (immediately)
- Confirm receipt
- What happens next (review timeline, call booking process)
- What to prepare for the call

### Pre-Call Email (24 hours before)
- Reminder with call link
- Agenda overview (3-4 topics)
- "Come prepared with [specific thing]"

### Post-Call Follow-Up (same day)
- Summary of what was discussed
- Proposed next steps
- Any resources mentioned on the call
- Clear timeline for decision

### Follow-Up if No Response (Day 3)
- Brief check-in
- Restate the key value point
- Easy reply option ("Reply 'yes' if you want to proceed, or 'not yet' if you need more time")

## Challenge Funnel (5-Day)

### Day 0: Welcome
- What the challenge is about
- What they will achieve by Day 5
- Day 1 assignment preview

### Days 1-4: Daily Emails
- Subject: "Day [N]: [Specific task]"
- Brief teaching (2-3 paragraphs)
- One clear action step
- Share progress prompt (community, reply, social)

### Day 5: Pitch
- Recap what they have achieved
- Bridge to the paid offer ("You have done [X] this week. The next step is [Y].")
- Clear CTA with offer details
- Deadline or incentive for fast action

## Email Writing Guidelines

### Subject Lines
- Under 50 characters
- Specific over clever
- Personalisation where genuine (not fake "[First Name]" if you do not have it)
- No ALL CAPS. No excessive punctuation.

### Body
- One idea per email
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- Mobile-first formatting
- Plain text outperforms heavy HTML for most business emails
- P.S. line for a secondary CTA or reminder (high readability spot)

### From Name
- Use a person's name, not a company name, where possible
- "[Name] from [Company]" works well
- Consistent across the sequence

### Send Times
- Test for your audience
- Business audiences: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am or 1-2pm local time
- Consumer audiences: evenings and weekends often perform better
- Consistency matters more than optimality
