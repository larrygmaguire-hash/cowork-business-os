# Natural Transitions

Transition phrases organised by function. Use these to improve readability and flow. Aim for 30%+ of sentences containing a transition word or phrase.

## Previewing Content
- What follows is...
- In the sections ahead...
- The next part covers...
- Before we get to that...

## Introducing Topics
- Consider this:
- Take, for example...
- One area worth examining is...
- There is a pattern here worth noting.

## Referring Back
- As mentioned earlier...
- Building on that point...
- This connects to...
- Returning to the earlier point about...

## Moving Between Sections
- With that in mind...
- Shifting focus now to...
- That brings us to...
- On a related note...

## Addition
- Beyond that...
- On top of that...
- What is more...
- Equally important...
- In addition to this...

## Contrast
- That said...
- On the other hand...
- Even so...
- Despite this...
- At the same time...
- And yet...

## Similarity
- In the same way...
- Along similar lines...
- This mirrors...
- Just as... so too...

## Cause and Effect
- As a result...
- Because of this...
- This leads to...
- The consequence is...
- What follows from this is...

## Examples
- For instance...
- To illustrate...
- A good example of this is...
- Consider the case of...

## Emphasis
- The key point here is...
- What matters most is...
- Above all...
- It is worth stressing that...

## Evidence
- Research shows that...
- According to...
- The data suggests...
- Evidence from [source] indicates...

## Summaries
- In summary...
- To put it simply...
- The takeaway here is...
- What this means in practice is...

## Conclusions
- Ultimately...
- When all is considered...
- The implication is clear:
- Looking at the bigger picture...

## Questions (as transitions)
- So what does this mean?
- Why does this matter?
- But how does this work in practice?
- What if the opposite were true?

## Lists and Series
- First... Second... Third...
- To begin with... Next... Finally...
- The first consideration is... Another is... A third is...

## Hedging Language
- It seems likely that...
- There is reason to think...
- This suggests, though does not prove...
- Perhaps the most telling sign is...

## Best Practices

- Place transitions mid-sentence or at natural connection points, not as standalone paragraph openers
- Vary your transitions — do not repeat the same one within 500 words
- Transitions should feel invisible. If the reader notices the transition, it is too heavy.
- Do not start paragraphs with "However," or "Moreover," or "Furthermore," — these read as formulaic AI writing

## Phrases to Avoid as Paragraph Openers

These are common AI tells when used to start paragraphs:

- However,
- Moreover,
- Furthermore,
- Additionally,
- In conclusion,
- It is important to note that...
- It is worth mentioning that...
- Interestingly,
- Notably,

Use them mid-sentence if needed. Never as the first word of a paragraph.
