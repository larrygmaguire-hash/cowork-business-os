# Article Writing

Workflow and structure for articles, blog posts, and newsletters.

## Article Workflow

1. **Clarify** — confirm platform, topic, angle, audience, sources
2. **Read voice** — consult `.claude/company/voice.md` for tone and formality
3. **Draft** — write using the 7-part structure below
4. **On-page SEO pass** — verify all checks from `on-page-seo.md`
5. **Voice and AI-tells pass** — run draft against `ai-tells.md`
6. **Readability pass** — verify Yoast-aligned standards from `editing-standards.md`
7. **Present for review** — deliver with word count and platform noted

## 7-Part Structure

### 1. Hook
Open with a specific fact, story, question, or observation. Not a definition. Not "In today's world..." The first sentence must earn the second sentence.

### 2. Context
Set the scene. Why does this topic matter now? What has changed? Who is affected? Ground the reader in the situation before presenting your argument.

### 3. Argument
State the core thesis clearly. One sentence if possible. This is what the article exists to say.

### 4. Evidence
Support the argument with data, examples, case studies, expert perspectives, or research. Every claim needs backing. If you cannot find evidence, state that explicitly rather than fabricating.

### 5. Nuance
Acknowledge complexity. Address counterarguments or limitations. This is where credibility is built. Articles that present only one side read as marketing, not thought leadership.

### 6. Application
Practical takeaways. How does the reader apply this? Specific steps, frameworks, or tools. The reader should leave the article knowing what to do differently.

### 7. Close
End with a forward-looking statement, a question, or a clear CTA. Not a summary of what was just said. The close should push the reader forward, not backward.

## Platform Adaptation

Adapt the structure and register to the platform:

| Platform | Register | Length | Structure Notes |
|----------|----------|--------|----------------|
| Company blog | Professional, accessible | 1,000-2,500 words | Full 7-part structure |
| Newsletter | Conversational, direct | 500-1,500 words | Can compress Context and Nuance |
| LinkedIn article | Professional, thought-leadership | 800-2,000 words | Strong hook essential, shorter paragraphs |
| Guest post | Match host publication's style | Per their guidelines | Research their existing content first |

## Voice Guidelines

- Read `.claude/company/voice.md` before every article
- The voice should feel like a knowledgeable person explaining something to a peer
- Avoid lecturing. Avoid preaching. Explain, argue, show.
- Weave sources into the narrative naturally ("Research from [institution] shows..." not "[Author, Year] found that...")
- First-person is appropriate when sharing direct experience. Third-person for general observations.

## Source Integration

- Integrate external sources into original prose — no plagiarism
- Direct quotes permitted with clear attribution when original phrasing is essential
- Paraphrase and synthesise in your own voice for everything else
- If a source cannot be verified, state "I could not verify this source" rather than citing it

## Dialogue and Quotes

- Use real quotes from interviews, research, or public statements
- Attribute clearly: "As [Name], [Role] at [Organisation], puts it: '...'"
- Do not fabricate quotes or attribute quotes to unnamed sources
- Internal dialogue (the reader's thoughts) can be used sparingly: "You are probably thinking: but what about...?"
