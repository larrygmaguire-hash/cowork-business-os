# Onboarding Guide 1 — Personal Preferences

**Where this applies:** Claude Desktop → Settings → General → Personal Preferences
**Time to complete with Claude:** ~15 minutes (or ~5 minutes using the Fast Path)

---

## What Personal Preferences Are

Personal Preferences is the account-wide profile Claude holds about **you, the human user**. It applies to every conversation you have on Claude Desktop and claude.ai — across every project, every chat, every session.

Anthropic's own guidance describes it as the place for your preferred approaches, the terminology you use, the typical scenarios you deal with, and your general communication preferences. In plain terms: the things Claude should always know about you before it answers a single question.

Personal Preferences is **not** the place for rules about how Claude should behave (that lives elsewhere — Cowork Global Instructions, or Project Instructions for a single project). Personal Preferences is about **who you are**.

### Where to find it

1. Open **Claude Desktop**.
2. Click your initials (bottom left).
3. Click **Settings**.
4. Click **General**.
5. Scroll to the **Personal Preferences** section.

The box accepts plain text. Whatever you paste in there is loaded into every conversation from that point on.

### Simple rule of thumb

- If a sentence starts with *"The user is…"* or *"I work on…"* → **Personal Preferences**.
- If a sentence starts with *"Always…"* or *"Never…"* → that's a behavioural rule and belongs somewhere else (we cover that in Onboarding Guide 2).

---

## How to Use This Document

1. Open Claude Desktop.
2. Start a **new conversation** (fresh chat window — not inside any Project, and not inside your Cowork Business OS workspace).
3. Copy everything below the line that says `--- PASTE FROM HERE ---` into the chat and send it.
4. Claude will interview you one question at a time.
5. At the end, Claude will produce a clean block of text in a code block.
6. Open **Settings → General → Personal Preferences**, paste the block in, save.

You do this once per machine, per user. Each person does their own.

### Two ways to complete this

- **Fast Path** — if you have a CV, a LinkedIn profile URL, a company "About" page, a staff bio, or any document that already describes you and your work, upload it or paste the URL at the start. Claude will read it, pull what it can, and only ask you about the gaps. This typically takes about five minutes.
- **Interview Path** — if you would rather start from scratch, skip the uploads and Claude will walk you through every question one at a time. This takes about fifteen minutes.

Both produce the same final block. Pick whichever suits you.

# Task — Help Me Write My Personal Preferences

You are helping me write the content for **Claude Desktop → Settings → General → Personal Preferences**. This is the account-wide profile about me that you will load into every conversation we have from now on.

## What Belongs in Personal Preferences

Anthropic's guidance is clear: Personal Preferences is for **account-wide settings that help you understand my general preferences so you can consider them in every response**. Specifically:

- My preferred approaches to work
- Terminology I use
- Typical scenarios I deal with
- General communication preferences
- Who I am, what I do, and the context I work in

**What does not belong here:**

- Rules about how you should behave (tone, formatting, language conventions) — those are separate
- Project-specific instructions — those live inside individual Projects
- Anything that only applies to one body of work

## How We Will Do This

Before you ask any questions, do this first:

**Step 0 — Offer the Fast Path.** Your first message to me must ask whether I want to use the Fast Path or the Interview Path. Phrase it like this:

> *"Before we start, do you have any material that already describes you and your work — a CV, a LinkedIn profile URL, a company 'About' page, a staff bio, or similar? If so, upload the file or paste the URL now and I will read it first, then only ask you about what is missing. If not, just say 'interview' and I will walk you through every question one at a time."*

Wait for my reply.

- **If I upload a file or give you a URL:** read it carefully. Extract everything relevant to the ten questions below (name, role, organisation, colleagues, typical work, stakeholders, terminology, location, background, context). Then tell me, in one short message, what you were able to pull from the source and which of the questions below you still need me to answer. Ask those remaining questions **one at a time**, in the order listed, skipping the ones the source already answered. Do not re-ask anything the source covered unless the source was ambiguous.
- **If I say "interview" or give you nothing usable:** proceed with every question below, one at a time.

Either way, once you reach the end, follow the "After the Final Answer" section exactly.

Now, the questions. Ask each of these **one at a time**, skipping any already answered by the uploaded source. Wait for my answer before moving on. One question per message. Do not batch questions. Do not offer examples unless I ask.

1. What is your full name, and what name do you prefer to be called?
2. What is your job title or role?
3. What organisation do you work for, and what does it do? If you give me a website, I will read it and extract the key facts myself rather than making you paraphrase.
4. Who are the 1–3 people you work with most closely? Names, roles, and one line each on the working relationship.
5. What are the main kinds of work you do in a typical week — the recurring tasks and the one-off ones?
6. Who are the main external stakeholders you interact with? (Examples: funders, regulators, partner organisations, clients, suppliers.)
7. What specialist terminology do you use in your work that I should recognise and use correctly? (Any sector acronyms, scheme names, internal jargon that carries meaning.)
8. What city and country do you work in? This sets my defaults for date formats, spelling, and legal context.
9. What is your background — the experience, training, or qualifications that shape how you approach your work?
10. Is there anything else about your working context I should always assume — sector norms, sensitivities, things not to presume about you or the people you work with?

## After the Final Answer

Produce a clean **Personal Preferences** block in plain text, written in the third person about me (e.g. *"The user is [Name], [Role] at [Organisation], a [short description of the organisation] based in [Location]…"*).

Structure it with short headings so it is easy to read and easy to update later:

```
## About Me
[name, role, background]

## My Organisation
[what it does, where it is, who it serves]

## Who I Work With
[close colleagues, external stakeholders]

## My Work
[main recurring tasks, typical scenarios]

## Terminology
[specialist terms I use]

## Context
[location, sector norms, anything else I should assume]
```

Keep it compact and factual. No flourishes, no marketing language, no filler. Put the whole block in a single code block so I can copy it cleanly.

## Final Step — Walk Me Through Saving It

After you produce the code block, stop and walk me through saving it. Do not assume I know where to click. Give me the steps one at a time and wait for me to confirm each one before moving on:

1. Tell me: *"Copy the block above — click inside the code block, select all, copy."* Wait for me to say done.
2. Tell me: *"Click your initials in the bottom-left corner of Claude Desktop."* Wait for me to say done.
3. Tell me: *"Click **Settings**."* Wait for me to say done.
4. Tell me: *"Click **General** in the settings menu."* Wait for me to say done.
5. Tell me: *"Scroll down to **Personal Preferences**."* Wait for me to say done.
6. Tell me: *"Paste the block into that field, then click Save (or whatever confirms the change)."* Wait for me to say done.
7. Confirm it is saved. Tell me to close Settings and start a new chat to test — I can ask you *"what do you know about me?"* and you should reflect the preferences back.

Do not skip steps. Do not combine steps. If I get stuck, help me before moving on.

## Rules for You During This Exercise

- One question per message. Wait for my answer.
- Plain English. No jargon.
- If I give you a website, fetch it and extract the relevant facts yourself.
- If an answer is vague, ask one short follow-up to sharpen it before moving on.
- Do not summarise my answers back to me after each question. Just move to the next one.
- No praise, no "great answer", no filler. Just the next question.
- At the end, produce the final block in a single code block.
