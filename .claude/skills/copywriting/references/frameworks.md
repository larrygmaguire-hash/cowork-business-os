# Copywriting Frameworks

Proven structures for common content formats. Pick the one that fits the purpose. None are magic -- they just prevent you from omitting key beats.

## AIDA — Awareness, Interest, Desire, Action

- **Attention:** hook the reader in the first line
- **Interest:** give them a reason to keep reading (a fact, a promise, a question)
- **Desire:** show how the thing changes their situation
- **Action:** tell them exactly what to do next

Use for: ads, landing pages, cold emails.

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

- **Problem:** name the pain the reader has
- **Agitate:** make them feel it (consequences, cost, frustration)
- **Solution:** present the answer

Use for: sales pages, direct-response content, any format where the reader's problem is well-defined.

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge

- **Before:** describe the reader's current state
- **After:** describe the transformed state
- **Bridge:** show what gets them from one to the other

Use for: case studies, testimonials, transformation-led marketing.

## FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits

- **Feature:** what the thing is or does
- **Advantage:** why that matters mechanically
- **Benefit:** what the reader gets

Use for: product pages, spec sheets, any context where readers need to evaluate a thing against alternatives.

## StoryBrand (condensed)

- A **character** has a **problem**
- They meet a **guide** (your brand) who gives them a **plan**
- That **calls them to action**, leading to **success** (or avoiding **failure**)

Use for: brand narratives, homepage content, founder letters.

## Inverted Pyramid (journalism)

- **Lead:** the most important facts first -- who, what, when, where, why
- **Body:** supporting details in descending order of importance
- **Tail:** background and context

Use for: news articles, announcements, press releases, any content where the reader may stop at any point.

## The Hook-Retain-Reward loop (long-form)

- **Hook:** pull the reader in within the first 2-3 sentences
- **Retain:** give them a reason to keep reading (tension, question, promise)
- **Reward:** deliver the payoff (insight, data, resolution)

Use for: blog posts, newsletters, YouTube scripts, essays.
