# Editing Standards

Baseline quality rules for any content produced or reviewed under the copywriting skill. Project-specific rules in `.claude/company/voice.md` override these.

## Clarity

- One idea per sentence. If a sentence has two ideas, split it.
- Prefer concrete nouns over abstractions. "Three customers cancelled" beats "some customer churn occurred."
- Replace adverbs with stronger verbs. "Walked quickly" becomes "strode."
- Remove filler phrases: "in order to" → "to", "due to the fact that" → "because", "at this point in time" → "now".

## Structure

- Lead with the most important point. Do not warm up.
- One paragraph per idea. Paragraphs longer than five sentences should be split.
- Use subheadings every 200-300 words in long-form content. Readers scan first, read second.
- End sections with a clear transition or a stated conclusion, not a cliffhanger.

## Voice

- Active voice by default. Use passive only when the actor is unknown or unimportant.
- Address the reader as "you" in direct-response content. Use first person sparingly.
- Match the tone to the format: landing pages are direct; blog posts are conversational; academic writing is formal.

## Evidence

- Claims of fact need a source. If you cannot cite, soften to opinion ("seems", "appears", "in my experience").
- Statistics must name the source in-line or in an adjacent sentence.
- Never fabricate numbers, quotes, or studies. If you cannot verify, state that the claim is unverified.

## Prohibited Patterns

- Marketing hype words without substance: "revolutionary", "game-changing", "best-in-class", "world-class", "cutting-edge".
- Hollow openers: "In today's fast-paced world", "Now more than ever", "Have you ever wondered".
- Disclaimer padding: "It is important to note that", "It should be mentioned that".
- Passive qualifiers: "arguably", "possibly", "it could be said that" -- pick a position.
