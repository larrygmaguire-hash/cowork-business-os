# SEO Checklist

Baseline technical and content requirements for any page or article. Project-specific keyword strategy in the page brief overrides these.

## On-Page Essentials

### Title tag
- 50-60 characters
- Primary keyword near the start
- One title tag per page
- Matches search intent, not brand copy

### Meta description
- 150-160 characters
- Includes the primary keyword
- Written as a pitch to click, not a summary
- Unique per page

### H1
- One per page
- Contains the primary keyword or a close variant
- Matches the title tag's topic but does not need to be identical

### H2, H3 subheadings
- Structure content hierarchically
- Use keyword variants and related terms
- Do not skip levels (no H3 without an H2 above it)

### URL slug
- Short, lowercase, hyphen-separated
- Contains the primary keyword
- No dates unless the content is time-dated
- No stop words where avoidable

### Body content
- Target keyword appears in the first 100 words
- Related keywords and variants spread through the body
- Minimum depth: 800 words for commentary, 1,500+ for a ranking attempt on competitive terms
- Internal links to 2-4 other relevant pages
- External links to 1-2 authoritative sources
- Images have descriptive file names and alt text

### Schema markup
- Article schema for blog posts
- FAQ schema for pages with Q&A sections
- Organization and WebSite schema on the homepage
- Breadcrumb schema on pages beyond the first level

## Technical

### Page speed
- Core Web Vitals: LCP under 2.5s, CLS under 0.1, INP under 200ms
- Compress images (WebP preferred)
- Lazy-load below-fold images
- Minify CSS/JS
- Use a CDN if serving globally

### Mobile
- Responsive layout -- no horizontal scrolling
- Tap targets at least 44 × 44 px
- Readable text without zooming
- No intrusive interstitials

### Crawlability
- Sitemap submitted to Google Search Console
- robots.txt not blocking important pages
- No orphan pages (every page linked from somewhere)
- Canonical tag on every page

### Security
- HTTPS site-wide
- No mixed content warnings

## Content Quality Signals

- Clear author attribution and credentials
- Date published and last updated (if relevant)
- Original research, data, or perspective -- not a summary of other pages
- Internal consistency (no contradictions across pages)
- Genuine answer to the user's query, not keyword padding

## E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust)

- Author pages with credentials and links to published work
- Citations for claims of fact
- Named sources for quotes
- Contact information on the site
- Social proof where relevant (testimonials with names, case studies with identifiable details)

## Search Intent Match

Before writing, classify the query:

| Intent | Format |
|--------|--------|
| Informational ("what is X") | Long-form article, definition, explainer |
| Navigational ("brand name") | Brand page, homepage, product page |
| Commercial ("best X for Y") | Comparison, review, category page |
| Transactional ("buy X", "X pricing") | Product page, pricing page, checkout-adjacent |

Mismatched content and intent will not rank regardless of other signals.

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Keyword stuffing (unnatural repetition)
- Thin content (under 300 words on a page trying to rank)
- Duplicate content (same page accessible via multiple URLs)
- Hidden text or cloaking
- Link schemes (paid backlinks, link exchanges, PBNs)
- Unattributed AI-generated content at scale
- Clickbait titles that do not match the body
