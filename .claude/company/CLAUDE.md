# Company

Six knowledge files that describe your business. Skills read these before producing any content, communications, or documents on your behalf.

## Files

| File | Contains | When skills read it |
|------|----------|---------------------|
| `overview.md` | What the business does, who it serves, value proposition | Before any content creation |
| `voice.md` | Brand voice, tone, formality, language preferences | Before any writing task |
| `brand.md` | Visual identity, colours, fonts, logos, asset locations | Before design work, presentations, branded documents |
| `audiences.md` | Primary and secondary audience profiles | Before content targeted at specific audiences |
| `team.md` | Key people, roles, collaborators | When referencing internal staff or assigning work |
| `industry.md` | Industry terminology, regulations, context | When industry-specific accuracy matters |

## How to populate

The `/setup` wizard asks about each file and writes the content based on your answers. You can also edit any file directly at any time -- skills pick up changes on the next task.

## How to update

Edit files directly when:
- Your business focus shifts (update `overview.md`)
- Your brand voice evolves (update `voice.md`)
- You add a new audience or refine existing ones (update `audiences.md`)
- Team changes (update `team.md`)
- Visual identity changes (update `brand.md`)

You can also re-run `/setup` and it will offer to update existing files.

## Why these files matter

Without populated company files, skills produce generic content. With them, skills produce content that sounds like your business, targets the right audience, uses the right terminology, and respects your brand. The richer these files are, the better the output.
